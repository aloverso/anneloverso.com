My Passions

Education and Curricular Design

I'm very passionate about engineering education - I teach math to grade-schoolers through our engineering outreach club. I am also involved in research on engineering education at Olin with Professor Yevgeniya Zastavker. Our research group focuses on studying what motivates students to pursue teaching-related careers during and after their time at Olin College.

This passion is what led me to take a semester-long Leave of Absence from Olin in Spring 2015 to pursue an opportunity designing Insper, a new engineering school in Brazil. That was an amazing experience and gave me incredible insights into teaching (especially internationally), education, and curricular design.

Advocating for Women in STEM

My first-year Media Activism class project was just one example of my activism in this area. As a woman in STEM, I had strong female role models, encouraging parents and teachers, and other important factors that many girls are not so lucky to have.

For my Girl Scout Silver Award project, I designed, organized, and ran an engineering workshop for local elementary-school girls, and the response was overwhelmingly positive. At Olin, I participate in the Gender and Engineering Co-Curricular group and have had many discussions about gender equality in engineering, both at Olin and outside of it.

Activities

One of my passions is circus arts. In 2007, I tried flying trapeze for the first time and have been going to TSNY Boston regularly since then learning flying trapeze. I've also trained with Simply Circus in Newton, MA for six years learning all other forms of circus - wirewalking, juggling, rolling globe, partner acrobatics, and more - but my passion is for aerials. At Simply Circus, I learned static trapeze, lyra, aerial fabrics, and cloudswing, and I've been developing my cloudswing skills intensely and performed in front of several hundred people at the First Night celebration in Boston.

I've also fallen in love with rock climbing. Another passion of mine is social dancing - on Olin's campus, I've learned blues dancing and introductions to swing. Also at Olin, I participate in the Human Powered Vehicles team, a club focused on building bikes from scratch for competition at ASME HPVC East.

Other

I'm a Girl Scout Gold Award recipient. In 2013, I completed my Gold Award project, which was focused on creating a curriculum for teaching aerial circus arts in school gym classes. I brought this to my community through circus outreach events to advocate for "unconventional" forms of exercise.

One constant hobby of mine is tinkering and mini coding projects. Right now, I have several projects in various stages of completion. The closest to finishing is a Python app for the card game Set, which I coded from scratch in Pygame and am now re-coding in Kivy to port to Android. I'm also making a music app for fans of my favorite band. Additionally, I love tinkering with my Raspberry Pi and my Leap Motion for fun mini projects and games.
